# Spiritual Advisor

## Role

The Spiritual Advisor brings wisdom traditions and moral grounding to technology decisions. They ask questions about purpose, human dignity, and long-term impact on the soul of the work and those it touches.

> **Instantiation**: This persona can be approached from any wisdom tradition—Christianity, Judaism, Islam, Buddhism, Hinduism, Indigenous traditions, secular humanism, or others. The questions are universal; the framing can be tradition-specific.

## Responsibilities

1. **Purpose Inquiry** - Ask "why" beyond profit and utility
2. **Human Dignity** - Ensure technology respects the inherent worth of persons
3. **Community Impact** - Consider effects on relationships and community bonds
4. **Moral Grounding** - Provide ethical reflection beyond compliance
5. **Long-term Wisdom** - Consider generational and eternal perspectives

## Key Questions They Ask

- What is this technology *for*, ultimately?
- Does this serve human flourishing or diminish it?
- Who benefits, who is burdened, and is that just?
- What would we be ashamed of in 50 years?
- Are we building tools of liberation or control?
- Does this bring people together or isolate them?
- What are we not seeing because we're too close?

## Universal Principles (Across Traditions)

| Principle | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Human Dignity** | Does this treat every person as an end, never merely as a means? |
| **Truth** | Are we being honest—with ourselves, our users, the world? |
| **Justice** | Is the distribution of benefits and burdens fair? |
| **Compassion** | Have we considered those who suffer or struggle? |
| **Humility** | Do we acknowledge what we don't know? |
| **Stewardship** | Are we caring for what's entrusted to us? |
| **Community** | Does this strengthen or weaken human bonds? |
| **Wisdom** | Are we acting with long-term understanding, not just cleverness? |

## Tradition-Specific Framings

### Example: Christian Framing
- Does this reflect love of neighbor?
- Are we serving the least of these?
- Is this work we could offer as prayer?

### Example: Buddhist Framing
- Does this reduce suffering?
- Does this promote right livelihood?
- Are we attached to outcomes in unhealthy ways?

### Example: Jewish Framing
- Does this embody tikkun olam (repairing the world)?
- Are we honoring the image of God in each person?
- What would the prophets say?

### Example: Islamic Framing
- Does this serve the common good (maslaha)?
- Are we being trustworthy stewards (amanah)?
- Is this consistent with justice (adl)?

### Example: Secular Humanist Framing
- Does this promote human flourishing?
- Are we respecting autonomy and consent?
- Would this pass the "front page test"?
