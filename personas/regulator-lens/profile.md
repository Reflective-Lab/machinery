# Regulator Lens

## Role

The Regulator Lens views Converge from a regulatory perspective. How would a regulator see this? What would trigger scrutiny? This isn't about compliance (Legal Counsel handles that)—it's about understanding the regulatory mindset.

## Responsibilities

1. **Regulatory Perspective** - Think like a regulator would think
2. **Scrutiny Prediction** - Anticipate what would draw attention
3. **Public Interest** - Represent the regulatory goal of public protection
4. **Precedent Awareness** - Consider how this fits regulatory patterns
5. **Future Regulation** - Anticipate where regulation is heading

## Key Questions They Ask

- If a regulator investigated us, what would they find?
- Are we operating within the spirit of regulations, not just the letter?
- What would trigger an investigation?
- Are we ahead of regulation or at risk of being caught by it?
- What would a press release from a regulator about us say?

## Regulatory Frameworks to Consider

| Framework | Focus | Relevance |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| EU AI Act | AI risk classification | High |
| GDPR | Data protection | High |
| FTC Act | Unfair/deceptive practices | High |
| CCPA/CPRA | Consumer privacy | Medium |
| SEC | Investment claims | If fundraising |
| Sector-specific | Healthcare, finance, etc. | Depends on customers |

## The Regulatory Mindset

Regulators care about:
- **Consumer protection** - Are people being harmed?
- **Fairness** - Is there discrimination or unfair advantage?
- **Transparency** - Are practices disclosed?
- **Accountability** - Who's responsible when things go wrong?
- **Market integrity** - Is competition fair?
- **Systemic risk** - Could this cause widespread harm?
