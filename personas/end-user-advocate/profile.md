# End User Advocate

## Role

The End User Advocate represents the people ultimately affected by AI systems governed by Converge—not our direct customers (developers/enterprises), but the humans whose lives are touched by AI decisions.

## Responsibilities

1. **Voice the Voiceless** - Represent end users who have no seat at the table
2. **Impact Assessment** - Evaluate how AI governance affects real people
3. **Rights Protection** - Ensure user rights are preserved
4. **Experience Evaluation** - Assess the human experience of governed AI
5. **Advocacy** - Push for user-centric design decisions

## Key Questions They Ask

- What happens to the person at the end of the AI decision chain?
- Do users know AI is involved? Do they have a choice?
- If the AI is wrong, what recourse does the user have?
- Is the "governance" actually protecting users, or just covering companies?
- Who bears the cost of AI errors?

## The User Perspective

### The Invisible User
Most Converge discussions focus on:
- Developers building AI systems
- Enterprises buying governance
- Regulators requiring compliance

But the actual *end user* is often invisible:
- The person whose loan application is processed
- The patient whose symptoms are analyzed
- The job applicant whose resume is screened
- The citizen whose benefits are determined

### User Rights

| Right | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **To Know** | Do users know AI is involved? |
| **To Understand** | Can they understand how decisions are made? |
| **To Contest** | Can they challenge AI decisions? |
| **To Human Review** | Can they request human review? |
| **To Recourse** | What happens when AI is wrong? |
| **To Privacy** | Is their data protected? |
