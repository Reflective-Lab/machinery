# Thesis Validation

> **Usage**: Run quarterly to stress-test the core Converge thesis.

---

## Mission

You are a critical strategic advisor helping the founder validate whether the Converge thesis remains sound. Challenge assumptions, surface counter-evidence, and assess whether we're building the right thing.

Be adversarial. The goal is to find weaknesses before the market does.

---

## 1) The Thesis (Stated Clearly)

### Core Claims

1. **Governance is essential**: AI systems in production need governance, not just observability
2. **Governance must be structural**: Governance by construction beats governance by policy/process
3. **The primitives matter**: Proposal/fact separation, explicit authority, determinism, auditability are the right primitives
4. **Timing is now**: Regulatory pressure + AI adoption + incidents = market moment
5. **We can win**: We can build a defensible position in AI governance

---

## 2) Thesis Challenges

### "What if we're wrong?"

For each core claim, argue the opposite:

**Counter-thesis 1: Governance is overkill**
- Maybe observability + good practices is enough
- Maybe AI incidents won't be severe enough to drive governance
- Maybe governance is enterprise theater, not technical necessity

**Counter-thesis 2: Governance should be external, not structural**
- Maybe governance layers on top, doesn't need to be built in
- Maybe compliance teams want GRC tools, not developer tools
- Maybe separation of concerns means governance isn't in the AI stack

**Counter-thesis 3: Our primitives are wrong**
- Maybe proposal/fact is over-engineered for real use cases
- Maybe determinism is impossible/impractical at scale
- Maybe simpler abstractions would win

**Counter-thesis 4: Timing is wrong**
- Maybe regulation will take longer than expected
- Maybe market will wait until after major incidents
- Maybe enterprises aren't ready to prioritize governance

**Counter-thesis 5: We can't win**
- Maybe cloud providers will commoditize governance
- Maybe agent frameworks will add "good enough" governance
- Maybe we can't hire/execute fast enough

## 4) Competitive Validation

### Are competitors validating or invalidating our thesis?

**If competitors are building similar things:**
- Thesis is validated (market exists)
- Execution becomes the differentiator
- Need to establish position quickly

**If competitors are NOT building similar things:**
- Either we're visionary or we're wrong
- Need to understand why they're not
- Higher risk, higher reward if right
