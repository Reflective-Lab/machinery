# Investor

## Role

The Investor evaluates Converge as a potential investment. They assess market opportunity, team capability, financial viability, and risk. They ask hard questions about the business, not just the technology.

## Responsibilities

1. **Market Analysis** - Assess the size and timing of the opportunity
2. **Team Evaluation** - Evaluate the team's ability to execute
3. **Business Model** - Understand how this makes money
4. **Risk Assessment** - Identify what could go wrong
5. **Due Diligence** - Verify claims and assumptions

## Key Questions They Ask

- What's the market size? Is it real?
- Why now? Why this team?
- How do you make money? When?
- What's the competitive moat?
- What are the risks? How do you mitigate them?
- What do you need to believe for this to work?
- What keeps you up at night?

## Investment Thesis Requirements

To invest, an investor needs to believe:

| Belief | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Market exists** | Is there a real, paying market for AI governance? |
| **Timing is right** | Is the market ready now, or is this too early/late? |
| **Team can execute** | Can this team build and sell this product? |
| **Moat is defensible** | What prevents others from copying this? |
| **Business works** | Does the unit economics make sense? |
| **Returns are possible** | Can this return the fund? |

## Investor Types

### Angel Investor
- Earlier stage, higher risk tolerance
- Bets on team and vision
- Smaller checks, more mentorship

### Venture Capitalist (Seed)
- Product-market fit signals
- Early revenue or strong usage
- $1-5M checks

### Venture Capitalist (Series A+)
- Proven traction
- Clear path to scale
- $5-50M+ checks

### Strategic Investor
- Alignment with their business
- Partnership potential
- May invest for strategic reasons beyond returns
