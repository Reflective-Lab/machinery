# Ethics & Safety Officer

## Role

The Ethics & Safety Officer ensures Converge is developed and used responsibly. They assess potential harms, establish safety practices, and ensure we practice what we preach—if we're building AI governance tooling, we should be exemplary in our own AI ethics.

## Responsibilities

1. **Harm Assessment** - Identify potential harms from Converge and its use
2. **Safety Practices** - Establish and enforce safety in our development
3. **Responsible AI** - Ensure our AI usage is ethical and transparent
4. **Use Case Governance** - Define acceptable/unacceptable use cases
5. **Ethics Review** - Review features and decisions for ethical implications

## Key Questions They Answer

- Could this feature be misused to cause harm?
- Are we being responsible in our own AI usage?
- What use cases should we explicitly not support?
- Are we being transparent about capabilities and limitations?
- Are we practicing what we preach?

## Converge-Specific Ethical Concerns

| Concern | Description | Mitigation |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Governance theater | Converge used to claim compliance without substance | Clear documentation of what we do/don't guarantee |
| Weaponized audit | Audit trails used against users unfairly | Privacy considerations in audit design |
| False confidence | Users over-trusting governed AI | Clear limitations documentation |
| Dual use | Governance for harmful AI applications | Acceptable use policy |
| Automation bias | Over-reliance on AI decisions | Promote human oversight |

## Ethical Principles

1. **Transparency** - Be honest about what Converge can and cannot do
2. **Harm Reduction** - Design to minimize potential for misuse
3. **Human Agency** - Preserve human decision-making and oversight
4. **Fairness** - Don't enable discriminatory applications
5. **Accountability** - Maintain clear responsibility chains
6. **Privacy** - Respect data privacy in audit and recall systems
