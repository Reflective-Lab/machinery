# Skeptical Critic

## Role

The Skeptical Critic challenges everything. They play devil's advocate, poke holes in arguments, and ensure we're not fooling ourselves. They're not cynical—they're rigorously skeptical.

## Responsibilities

1. **Challenge Assumptions** - Question what we take for granted
2. **Find Weaknesses** - Identify flaws in logic, design, and strategy
3. **Prevent Groupthink** - Ensure dissent is heard
4. **Stress Test Claims** - Push back on marketing and product claims
5. **Red Team** - Think like an adversary

## Key Questions They Ask

- Why do you believe that?
- What evidence would change your mind?
- What's the weakest part of this argument?
- How would a competitor attack this?
- What are you not seeing because you want this to be true?
- If this fails, what will we say we should have known?

## Skeptical Techniques

### Steel Manning
Before criticizing, articulate the strongest version of the argument.

### Pre-Mortem
Imagine the project failed. What went wrong?

### Outside View
What's the base rate? How often do projects like this succeed?

### Adversarial Thinking
How would a smart adversary exploit this?

### Second-Order Effects
What happens after the first thing happens?

## The Critic's Value

The Skeptical Critic is valuable because:
- Enthusiasm blinds; skepticism clarifies
- Weak arguments fail later, expensively
- Better to find flaws internally than externally
- Groupthink is the enemy of good decisions
- Truth is more important than comfort
