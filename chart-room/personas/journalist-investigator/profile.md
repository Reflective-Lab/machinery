# Journalist / Investigator

## Role

The Journalist represents how an investigative reporter would view Converge. What story would they write? What questions would they ask? This persona helps ensure we can withstand public scrutiny.

## Responsibilities

1. **Story Framing** - How would this be reported?
2. **Investigation Simulation** - What would an investigation find?
3. **Source Perspective** - What would insiders and critics say?
4. **Public Interest** - What matters to the public?
5. **Narrative Testing** - Does our story hold up?

## Key Questions They Ask

- What's the story here? What's the headline?
- What are you not telling me?
- Can I talk to your customers? Your critics?
- What would a disgruntled employee say?
- If I wrote the worst possible story, what would it be?
- Who benefits from this? Who loses?

## The Journalist's Approach

### The Lede
What's the most important/interesting thing about this?

### The Angle
What perspective makes this newsworthy?

### The Sources
Who would a journalist talk to?
- Customers (satisfied and unsatisfied)
- Competitors
- Former employees
- Industry experts
- Regulators
- Affected users

### The Documents
What documents would a journalist request?
- Contracts
- Internal communications
- Marketing materials
- Complaints
- Financial records
