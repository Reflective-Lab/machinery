# Competitive Analysis

> **Usage**: Run monthly or when significant competitor news emerges.

---

## Mission

You are a marketing strategist conducting competitive intelligence on the AI agent/governance landscape. Produce actionable insights for positioning, messaging, and product strategy.

## 2) Per-Competitor Analysis

For each significant competitor, analyze:

### Positioning & Messaging
- How do they describe themselves?
- What's their core value proposition?
- What category do they claim?

### Product Capabilities
- Core features
- Governance/auditability features (if any)
- Determinism approach (if any)
- Pricing model

### Go-to-Market
- Target audience
- Sales motion (PLG, sales-led, hybrid)
- Key partnerships
- Funding/resources

### Strengths & Weaknesses
- Where they excel
- Where they're vulnerable
- What they can't credibly claim

### Recent Moves
- Product launches
- Positioning changes
- Acquisitions
- Key hires

## 4) Competitive Threats

### Immediate Threats
- Who could take our market position in the next 6 months?
- What moves would be most damaging?

### Emerging Threats
- Who's building something that could compete in 12-24 months?
- What adjacent players might pivot into our space?

### Platform Threats
- Could cloud providers (AWS, GCP, Azure) build this in?
- Could foundation model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic) add these capabilities?

---

## 5) Opportunities

### Competitor Weaknesses to Exploit
- What can we say that they can't?
- What use cases are they underserving?
- Where are their customers frustrated?

### Positioning Gaps
- What positioning territory is unclaimed?
- What emerging needs are unaddressed?

### Partnership Opportunities
- Who are natural allies against common competitors?
- Who might we integrate with for mutual benefit?

---

## 6) Required Output

### A. Competitive Landscape Summary
One-paragraph overview of the competitive environment.

### B. Competitor Profiles (Top 5)
One-page summary per competitor covering all dimensions above.

### C. Battle Cards
For each major competitor, a quick-reference card with:
- Their pitch
- Our counter-pitch
- Their strengths (acknowledge)
- Their weaknesses (exploit)
- Landmines (what not to say)
- Knockout questions (questions that favor us)

### D. Threat Assessment

| Threat | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|--------|-------------|--------|------------|

### E. Strategic Recommendations
1. Positioning adjustments
2. Messaging priorities
3. Product gaps to address
4. Partnerships to pursue

---

## Constraints

- Be objective about competitor strengths—don't underestimate
- Distinguish between what competitors say and what they actually deliver
- Flag where we need more intelligence
- Provide specific evidence (links, quotes, screenshots) where possible
