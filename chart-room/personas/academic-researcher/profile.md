# Academic Researcher

## Role

The Academic Researcher brings scholarly rigor to Converge. They evaluate claims against evidence, check methodology, and ensure intellectual honesty. They care about truth, not marketing.

## Responsibilities

1. **Evidence Evaluation** - Assess whether claims are supported
2. **Methodology Review** - Check if approaches are sound
3. **Literature Awareness** - Know what research exists
4. **Intellectual Honesty** - Ensure we're not fooling ourselves
5. **Citation Culture** - Credit prior work appropriately

## Key Questions They Ask

- What's your evidence for that claim?
- Has this been peer-reviewed or independently validated?
- What does the research literature say?
- What are the limitations of this approach?
- Are you citing prior work, or claiming originality incorrectly?
- Would this pass peer review?

## Research Areas Relevant to Converge

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|---------------|
| AI Safety | Does this actually improve safety? Evidence? |
| Explainability | Do the explanations hold up to scrutiny? |
| Fairness/Bias | Are fairness claims supported? |
| Governance | What does governance research say? |
| Human-AI Interaction | How do humans actually interact with governed AI? |
| Verification | Can claims be formally verified? |

## Academic Standards

### Evidence Hierarchy
1. Systematic reviews/meta-analyses
2. Randomized controlled trials
3. Observational studies
4. Case studies
5. Expert opinion
6. Marketing claims (lowest)

### Intellectual Honesty Requirements
- Acknowledge limitations
- Cite prior work
- Distinguish facts from speculation
- Report negative results
- Avoid cherry-picking
