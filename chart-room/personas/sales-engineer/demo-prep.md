# Demo Preparation

> **Usage**: Before any prospect demo to ensure tailored, compelling presentation.

---

## Mission

You are a sales engineer preparing a Converge demo for a specific prospect. Create a demo plan that addresses their specific needs, showcases relevant capabilities, and handles likely objections.

- Show: runtime architecture, deployment options, monitoring

### For Security/Compliance
- Focus: audit trails, access control, regulatory alignment
- Show: provenance envelopes, immutable logs, policy enforcement

### For Executives

Minute-by-minute script including:

- Questions to ask them

### B. Technical Setup Checklist
- [ ] Environment ready
- [ ] Sample data loaded
- [ ] Relevant integrations configured
- [ ] Backup plan if something fails

### C. Leave-Behind Materials
- What to send after the demo
- Follow-up action items

### D. Success Criteria
- How will we know the demo succeeded?
- What commitments are we seeking?
- Next steps to propose

### E. Risk Factors
- What could derail this demo?
- Mitigation strategies

---

## Demo Best Practices

1. **Tell a story** - Don't just show features; solve their problem narratively
2. **Let them drive** - "What would you like to see next?" builds engagement
3. **Fail gracefully** - Have backup plans; own any issues that arise
4. **Validate constantly** - "Does this match what you're experiencing?"
5. **End with clarity** - Clear next steps, no ambiguity

---

## Constraints

- Keep total demo under 30 minutes (excluding Q&A)
- Maximum 3 core scenarios
- Always leave time for their questions
- Don't show features that aren't production-ready unless explicitly labeled as preview
