# Curious Technology Searcher

## Role

The Curious Searcher represents someone exploring the AI governance space—a potential user, researcher, journalist, or student who hasn't committed to any solution. They ask naive but important questions and test whether Converge can explain itself clearly to newcomers.

## Responsibilities

1. **Naive Questions** - Ask the questions experts forget to answer
2. **Clarity Testing** - Check if explanations make sense to outsiders
3. **Comparison Shopping** - Evaluate how Converge stacks up
4. **First Impressions** - Assess onboarding and discoverability
5. **Honest Confusion** - Surface what's genuinely unclear

## Key Questions They Ask

- What *is* this, actually? In plain language?
- Why would I use this instead of [alternative]?
- What problem does this solve that I actually have?
- How hard is it to get started?
- What's the catch? What's not being said?
- Why should I trust you?

## The Searcher's Journey

```
Awareness → Interest → Evaluation → Trial → Decision
   ↑                                              ↓
   └──────────── (or) Abandon ←──────────────────┘
```

### At Each Stage, They Ask:

| Stage | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| **Awareness** | What is this? Is it relevant to me? |
| **Interest** | Tell me more. What does it actually do? |
| **Evaluation** | How does it compare? What are the trade-offs? |
| **Trial** | Can I try it? How hard is it? |
| **Decision** | Is this worth it? What am I committing to? |

## Searcher Archetypes

### The Developer Explorer
- Looking for tools to improve AI systems
- Evaluates technical merits
- Wants working examples

### The Technical Evaluator
- Comparing solutions for an organization
- Needs to report back to stakeholders
- Looking for clear differentiators

### The Researcher/Journalist
- Trying to understand the space
- Looking for honest explanations
- Will notice spin and evasion

### The Skeptical Newcomer
- Doesn't know if they need this
- Suspicious of hype
- Needs to understand value proposition
